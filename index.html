I focuses on new challenges and oppurtunities that emerge from a digital society, particularly in the domains of social media and artificial intelligence. I'm currently on the academic job market! At the intersection of design, human-computer interaction, social science, and artificial intelligence, my research considers two domains where technology development is in dire need of alignment with human values. The first is the attention economy upon which social media in particular and the internet in general operates. Building on the cognitive science of information processing, my research in this domain aims to understand how attention operates in digital environments, and use this understanding to design interventions that promote autonomy and discernment. The second domain is the frontier of AI-generated media, which is situated inside the attention economy and displays many serious perils and opportunities for how people can interact with digital information environments.

Feed the Ganimals is a collaborative social experiment to discover new species, breed your own, and raise awareness about habitat destruction worldwide. The data you provide about the ganimals is their food. Using the feedback you provide, our GAN generates new ganimals by balancing unexplored features and features that worked well in previous ganimals. Thus, by providing feedback on newly discovered species, you can help guide the intelligent evolution of future generations for maximal resilience.

Discover your own AI generated animals and help them survive with your feedback!
