Zivvy Epstein


I am a PhD student in the MIT Media Lab, where I'm co-advised by David Rand and Sandy Pentland. My work integrates aspects of design and computational social science to understand and build cooperative systems. I focuses on new challenges and oppurtunities that emerge from a digital society, particularly in the domains of social media and artificial intelligence.

 twitter  email  cv  scholar  

My research centers around creating new technologies and insights that make the internet a better place. Recently, this has manifested as design interventions to reduce misinformation on social media. I believe that many of the ills of social media and the modern web stem from myopic and profit-first design, which can be mitigated through awareness, cooperation and unorthodox experience design. But the epistemologies of quantitative research can only go so far. To explore these concepts through a more qualitative and playful lens, I also create digital art that has been showcased and covered at such venues as Ars Electronia, FastCo, MIT News and Digg.


Feed the Ganimals is a collaborative social experiment to discover new species, breed your own, and raise awareness about habitat destruction worldwide. The data you provide about the ganimals is their food. Using the feedback you provide, our GAN generates new ganimals by balancing unexplored features and features that worked well in previous ganimals. Thus, by providing feedback on newly discovered species, you can help guide the intelligent evolution of future generations for maximal resilience.

Discover your own AI generated animals and help them survive with your feedback!
