Zivvy Epstein is a PhD student in the MIT Media Lab, where he is co-advised by David Rand and Sandy Pentland. His work integrates aspects of design and computational social science to model and understand cooperative systems. He focuses on new challenges and oppurtunities that emerge from a digital society, particularly in the domains of artificial intelligence and social media.

Research

His research centers around creating new technologies and insights to make the internet a better place for everyone. Recently, this has manifested as design interventions to reduce misinformation on social media. His work suggests that many of the ills of socia media and the modern web stem from myopic and profit-first design, which can be mitigated through increased awareness, cooperation and reflection.

Projects

Feed the Ganimals is a collaborative social experiment to discover new species, breed your own, and raise awareness about habitat destruction worldwide. The data you provide about the ganimals is their food. Using the feedback you provide, our GAN generates new ganimals by balancing unexplored features and features that worked well in previous ganimals. Thus, by providing feedback on newly discovered species, you can help guide the intelligent evolution of future generations for maximal resilience.

Discover your own AI generated animals and help them survive with your feedback!
