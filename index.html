Ziv Epstein (he/him) is a PhD student in the MIT Media Lab, where he is co-advised by David Rand and Sandy Pentland. His primary research integrates design and computational social science to understand and build the next generation of social computing tools. In particular, his research explores ways to conceptualize, measure, and shift attention online. He does so by developing interventions against misinformation, as well as exploring the role of algorithms such as newsfeed recommenders and generative AI in social interactions online. He built a platform called Yourfeed for ecologically valid social media research.

He has published papers in venues such as the general interest journals Nature, PNAS and iScience, as well as top-tier computer science proceedings such as Computer Human Interaction (CHI) and Computer Supported Community Work (CSCW). His work has also received widespread media attention in outlets like the New York Times, Scientific American, and Fast Company. He is also a practicing multimedia artist whose work has been featured in Ars Electronica, the MIT Museum, and Burning Man.
